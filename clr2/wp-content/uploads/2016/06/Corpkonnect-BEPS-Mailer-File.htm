As a G20 nation, India has played an active role the Base Erosion and Profit Shifting (BEPS) project, working closely with the Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development (OECD) officials in preparing rules and participating in deliberations. OECD has issued 15 Actions Plan as part of it’s work on Base Erosion and Profit Shifting. India’s commitment to adopt and implement BEPS was seen in forward direction with the announcement of India`s tax reporting scenario - Country-by-Country Reporting (`CbCR`) in the Union budget 2016. Also, the recently concluded amendment in the India Mauritius Tax Protocol and the introduction of a equalization levy have further supported to the fact that India’s is taking its steps forward in curbing abuse of Tax Treaties and taxation in digital economies, two of the major pillars of BEPS Action plans.The biggest concern in the implementation of BEPS is the low level of awareness in the taxmen on how the BEPS measures will impact business operations and the need for increased compliance.
 

CorpKonnect’s full day BEPS & International Taxation Masterclass will identify and throw light on various issues and problems related to the implementation of various BEPS Action plans in the companies and also the fulfill the knowledge gap in compliance framework to be followed to adhere to the new requirements.
